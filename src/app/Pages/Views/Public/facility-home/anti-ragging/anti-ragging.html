Anti Ragging Policy

Zero Tolerance Policy

CIPC has a strict zero-tolerance policy against ragging. Any form of ragging is strictly prohibited and will result in severe disciplinary action, including expulsion from the institution.

Ragging is a criminal offense under the UGC Regulations on Curbing the Menace of Ragging in Higher Educational Institutions, 2009.

What is Ragging?

Ragging includes any act that causes or is likely to cause physical or psychological harm, fear, or apprehension in a student.

  • Physical abuse or assault
  • Mental harassment or humiliation
  • Financial extortion
  • Any act that violates dignity

Report Ragging

If you witness or experience any form of ragging, report it immediately to the Anti-Ragging Squad or the authorities.
